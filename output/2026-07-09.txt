## 1. Top AI News (5–7 stories)
1. **Character.AI's Interactive Productions**: Character.AI has launched its own microdrama productions, allowing users to interact with characters, ask questions, and roleplay storylines. This matters because it blurs the line between content consumption and creation, offering a new level of engagement and potentially redefining the entertainment industry.
2. **Ollama's $65M Raise**: Ollama, an open-source AI developer tool, has raised $65M and grown to nearly 9M users. This is significant because it demonstrates the growing demand for accessible AI development tools, empowering more developers to work with AI without extensive resources.
3. **Google's Deepfake Detector**: Google's deepfake detector was used to debunk a hoax picture of Senator Mitch McConnell. This is crucial as it showcases the real-world application and necessity of AI in combating misinformation and fake media.
4. **Fundamentum's $200M Fund**: Nandan Nilekani has left his GP role at Fundamentum as the firm launches a $200M third fund targeting AI and fintech startups in India. This matters because it indicates a significant investment in the future of AI and fintech in India, which could lead to innovative solutions and growth in the region.
5. **OpenAI's Wealth Sharing Promise**: OpenAI CEO Sam Altman's promise of sharing AI-created wealth with Americans has been highlighted, with potential implications for how AI benefits are distributed. This has real-world impact as it raises questions about the equitable distribution of AI-generated wealth and its potential to reshape economic structures.
